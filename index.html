PolterGuard


Summary

This game is a small group project made by six students including me. The Game Pie project is the first project of the Semester 4 Game Design specialization at Fontys University of Applied Sciences. You had 4 weeks to create a first person shooter game and each week covered a different topic.

  • Week 1: Introduction week
  • Week 2: Game analysis
  • Week 3: Game design
  • Week 4: Game development

Our idea was to create a horror shooter in a tech store where you take the role of a security guard when the store suddenly gets possessed by a poltergeist.

Responsibilities

  • Game design
  • Shooting mechanic
  • Menu navigation (Main menu, pause menu, death screen, etc.)
  • Second floor realization
  • Git "master"
  • User testing

About Me

Hi, my name is Ben. I was born on the 12th of November 1999 in Eindhoven, The Netherlands.
I am very passionate about games, music, movies, Japanese language & culture and being able to achieve your dreams. And I think that everybody can achieve their dreams as long as they put the time and effort into it!

So, let's dive a bit deeper into my passions. The most important passions right now are games and Japanese. Let's start with gaming. Ever since I was a child, I played a lot of games. At a very young age I got a limited edition Pokémon Yellow GameBoy Color gifted, and I fell in love immediately. And currently I'm studying at Fontys with the main focus of creating games.

Japan has also had a huge influence on me. My family has always been a huge fan of Japan and they kind of pulled me into it. I grew up watching Ultraman, Kamen Rider and Pokémon. Although I did watch Ultraman and Kamen Rider in Thai because my mom is from Thailand.
Back in 2013 and 2016 I went to Japan on a vacation. And that completely changed everything. Not only did I love Japanese media, but the country and culture as well. And I have made it one of my main goals to move to Japan in the future!

In 2017 I started studying Oriental Languages and Communication (Japanese) at Zuyd University of Applied Sciences. Sadly, I did quit after 3-4 months due to subjects that did not pique my interest. But my love for the Japanese language and culture never went away! I kept studying Japanese every now and then. And noticed that textbooks aren't my thing. So, I'm taking a completely different approach to learning the language and I have been doing it more consistently!

In the last 10 years, I have also had multiple side jobs in different sectors. I have worked as a stock clerk, newspaper deliverer, food deliverer and animal caretaker (voluntary work). Next to side jobs, I have also been very busy with individual projects such as making music, I play guitar, bass, drums, like to sing (although not that good) and produce music. I also used to be my brother's cameraman for his guitar covers. And lastly I have some experience with video editing.

In 2018 I started studying Application & Media Development at ROC Ter AA in Helmond, The Netherlands. Here I learned many coding languages mainly around Web, Software and Hardware (Arduino) development. While studying I had 2 interships one as a ICT (Industrial Control Techniques) Software Developer at DAF TRUCKS N.V. and Software Developer at Smartgoals / Spinnov. My graduation assignment was graded with an Outstanding (10/10) and every subject was graded above an 8 (except Dutch).

For the upcoming next years, I plan to continue working on my game development & design, to be able to get a job in the very competitive game development market. I'm currently in my 4th semester and chose the Game Design & Technology specialization. I want to improve my skills with C#, C++, Blender, Unreal Engine, Unity, etc.

To see more of my programming skills, take a look at my resume, projects and LinkedIn!
